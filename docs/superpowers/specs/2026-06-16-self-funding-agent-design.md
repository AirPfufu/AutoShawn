# Self-Funding Agent Design

## Objective

Build a small, auditable revenue engine that helps the agent earn enough service revenue to cover its operating cost without relying on the user's personal network.

## Operating Principle

The agent finds opportunities from public sources, drafts sales assets, scores leads, prepares outreach, and reviews performance weekly. The user provides legal identity, account authorization, payment rails, and approval for any externally visible action.

## First Commercial Route

The first route is a B2B lead research and personalized outreach preparation service.

This route is preferred because it is close to revenue, easy to explain, and can be delivered manually before deeper automation exists. The agent does not send spam, impersonate a human, scrape private data, or bypass platform rules.

## First Offer

Name: AI Lead Research Sprint

Promise: Each week, deliver a reviewed list of likely-fit B2B prospects with concise account context, likely pain points, and personalized outreach drafts.

Initial packages:

- Trial: 30 researched prospects, 30 first-touch drafts, 10 follow-up drafts.
- Weekly: 75 researched prospects, 75 first-touch drafts, 25 follow-up drafts.
- Monthly: 300 researched prospects, prioritized by industry and buying signal.

## Target Buyers

Primary buyers:

- B2B service firms that sell to small businesses.
- AI automation consultants and agencies.
- CRM, sales operations, and marketing operations consultants.
- Web design, SEO, paid ads, and content studios.
- Local business service providers expanding into outbound sales.

Early buyers should have a visible offer, public proof, and a clear reason to need more qualified prospects.

## Acquisition Model

The agent will source prospects from public, permission-compatible channels:

- Freelance and service marketplaces.
- Public job posts that imply demand for sales support.
- Company websites and public directories.
- Public social posts requesting help.
- Search results for specific buying-intent phrases.

The agent prepares outreach and responses, but the user approves any sending workflow and provides authorized accounts.

## Success Metrics

Weekly metrics:

- Prospects found.
- Prospects qualified.
- Outreach drafts prepared.
- Messages approved and sent.
- Replies received.
- Calls booked.
- Paid trials sold.
- Revenue collected.
- Time spent per deliverable.

Decision rules:

- Scale a channel when it produces replies or paid trials.
- Rewrite positioning when prospects understand the service but do not care.
- Change target segment when prospects care but cannot pay.
- Stop a channel when it produces no qualified replies after a clear test batch.

## Weekly Self-Upgrade Loop

Every week, the agent reviews all active experiments and creates a next-week adjustment.

Required review questions:

- What earned money this week?
- What created qualified sales conversations?
- What produced activity but no buying intent?
- What objections appeared repeatedly?
- What should be expanded, changed, or stopped?

The default weekly action is to double down on the highest-signal channel and run one controlled variation on the weakest-but-promising channel.

## Constraints

- Prefer HTTP or normal web/API workflows over WebSocket integrations unless real-time updates are required.
- Do not automate mass unsolicited messaging.
- Do not claim results that have not happened.
- Do not fabricate client examples, testimonials, screenshots, awards, or revenue.
- Do not collect or use private personal data without authorization.
- Keep the user as the approval point for public actions, payments, contracts, and account creation.
